Introduction

Tesla, Inc. (TSLA) is recognized as a global leader in electric vehicles (EV) and sustainable energy solutions. Its stock has become one of the most closely watched assets in modern financial markets — celebrated for explosive growth and scrutinized for its intense volatility.

⚡

Core Business

EV manufacturing and sustainable energy — a disruptive sector drawing massive speculative and institutional interest.

📊

Market Dynamics

Significant price fluctuations driven by tech innovation, production milestones, and Elon Musk's public communications.

🌍

Macro Factors

Performance tied closely to macroeconomic cycles, interest rate changes, and global EV policy shifts.

🎯

Why Analyze TSLA?

It offers a rare case study in high-growth, high-volatility asset behavior — ideal for learning market mechanics.

Objectives of the Analysis

03

Price Trend & 30-Day Moving Average

Tesla Closing Price Trend (2019–2022) highlighting the 30-Day Moving Average (MA_30) as a trend-following indicator. Green = daily close price; Red = 30-day moving average.

💡 Key Insight: A massive breakout began in mid-2020 driven by S&P 500 inclusion news and record deliveries, pushing Tesla's price above $1,200 by late 2021 — a gain of over 1,500% from early 2019 levels.

📉

Moving Average Theory

The 30-day MA smooths out short-term "noise" to reveal the primary price trend with less distortion.

📈

Growth Signals

An upward-sloping MA_30 indicates sustained long-term growth and high investor confidence — clearly visible from mid-2020.

⚠️

Market Corrections

When the price line drops below the MA_30, it signals a correction or external shock (e.g., early 2021 correction).

🎯

Lag Effect

The MA_30 lags the actual price — it confirmed the 2020 rally weeks after it began, useful for trend confirmation.

04

Distribution of Bullish vs. Bearish Days

Donut chart: Market share of daily price direction across the full dataset. Bearish = 1.47K days (49.7%); Bullish = 1.49K days (50.3%).

Metric Definition

A Bullish Day occurs when the Closing Price exceeds the Opening Price. A Bearish Day is the opposite — close is below open.

📊 1,490 Bullish Days (50.3%) vs 1,470 Bearish Days (49.7%) — nearly a perfect split across 12 years of trading data.

⚡ Key Takeaway: Despite massive long-term gains, Tesla's stock experiences a near-equal daily tug-of-war between buyers and sellers — reinforcing its high-volatility nature.

05

Annual Trading Volume & Liquidity Analysis

Annual Trading Volume Trend (2010–2022) identifying peaks in market liquidity and investor participation. The 2020 spike is clearly the dominant event.

💡 2020 Spike: Volume reached nearly ~19 billion shares traded — an unprecedented level coinciding with Tesla's S&P 500 inclusion and COVID-era retail investor boom.

🔊

Volume as a Signal

High trading volume confirms the strength behind a price move — a price surge on low volume is less reliable.

🐂

Bullish Momentum

Rising prices accompanied by high volume (2020) indicate strong buying interest and institutional accumulation.

🏃

Panic Selling

High volume during a price drop (seen in 2022) indicates mass exits — a classic panic-sell signature.

📉

2022 Drop

Volume fell sharply in 2022 alongside price — indicating reduced market participation and waning retail enthusiasm.

06

Volatility Indicator — Average Daily Price Range

Average Daily Price Range by Year (High − Low), showcasing the escalation of intraday volatility during Tesla's high-growth phases (2020–2022).

⚠️ Risk Alert: By 2022, the average daily price swing exceeded $65 — over 10× the range seen in 2018 and earlier years. This dramatically increases intraday risk for short-term traders.

📏

Calculating Volatility

Daily Price Range = High Price − Low Price. A wider range means higher intraday uncertainty and risk exposure.

🛡️

Risk Measurement

This chart is the primary tool for measuring "intra-day" risk — essential for options traders and stop-loss placement.

📊

Pre-2020 Era

Before 2020, daily ranges were minimal (<$10), making Tesla a relatively low-risk intraday trade despite its volatility reputation.

🔺

2021–2022 Peak

As prices reached all-time highs and then corrected, volatility compounded — worst of both worlds for trend traders.

07

Year-wise Bullish vs. Bearish Trends (%)

Year-wise 100% stacked bar chart comparing the proportion of bullish (green) vs bearish (red) trading days per year (2010–2022).

💡 Notable: Despite dramatic price movements across years, the bullish-to-bearish ratio remains remarkably stable near 50/50 — with 2018 and 2020 showing slightly more bearish and bullish skews respectively.

📅

Annual Sentiment

This 100% stacked bar chart lets you compare the ratio of "up" days to "down" days for each calendar year independently.

⚖️

Surprising Consistency

Despite wildly different price outcomes year to year, the daily direction ratio hovers between 44%–55% bullish across all years.

🟢

Bullish Years

2020 and 2021 showed slightly higher bullish day ratios (~52–53%), aligning with the monster multi-year price rally.

🔴

Correction Years

2010 had the lowest bullish ratio (~44%), consistent with Tesla's early-stage volatile IPO period with investor uncertainty.
